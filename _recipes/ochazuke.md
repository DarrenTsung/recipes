---
title: Ochazuke
---

Adapted from: https://www.justonecookbook.com/ochazuke/

## Ingredients:

- 1 fillet [baked salmon](/baked_salmon), flaked into bite-size pieces
- 1 cup cooked Japanese short-grain rice
- 1 tsp bubu arare (crispy puffed rice pellets) or crushed Japanese rice crackers
- 1 tsp shredded nori seaweed
- ¼ tsp toasted white sesame seeds
- 2 sprigs mitsuba (or ⅛ scallion, cut into small pieces)
- Wasabi (optional)

### For Dashi:

- 1 cup dashi
- 1 tsp mirin
- 1 tsp soy sauce
- ⅛ tsp kosher salt

### For Green Tea Version:

- 2 tsp Japanese green tea leaves
- 1 cup hot water
- ½ tsp soy sauce (optional)

## Instructions:

1. **Prepare the liquid:**
   - For dashi: Combine dashi, mirin, soy sauce, and salt. Bring to a boil.
   - For tea: Steep tea leaves in hot water for 1-2 minutes.

2. **Assemble:**
   - Place rice in a bowl.
   - Top rice with flaked salmon.
   - Sprinkle with bubu arare, nori, and sesame seeds.

3. **Serve:**
   - Pour hot dashi or tea over rice.
   - Garnish with mitsuba or scallion.
   - Add wasabi if desired.

## Notes:

- Can be served hot or cold.
- Summertime variation: use cold rice and cold dashi/tea.
