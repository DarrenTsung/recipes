## Notes

Super lazy version â€” boil meat, rinse, then combine everything in a pot. Neck bones are cheaper but have a worse bone-to-meat ratio. 

## Ingredients:

- 3 lbs beef neck bones or oxtail
- 2 tbsp oil
- 2 in piece ginger, smashed / cut and not peeled.
- 6 cloves garlic, smashed and not peeled.
- 3 scallions, rinsed and twisted in half.
- 1 onion, cut into half. Not peeled.
- 2-3 tomatoes, cut into half
- 2 Thai chilis
- 1 tbsp tomato paste
- 2 tbsp doubanjiang
- 2 tsp sugar
- 1/2 cup soy sauce
- 1/2 cup Shaoxing wine
- 8 cups water
- 1 [lu bao](https://thewoksoflife.com/chinese-spice-braising-packets/) packet

### For Serving:

- ~220g fresh wheat noodles
- Bok choy
- Cilantro, chopped
- Scallions, chopped
- Chinese pickled mustard greens

## Instructions:

1. Blanch beef in boiling water for 1 minute. Rinse thoroughly.
2. Combine beef, oil, ginger, garlic, scallions, onion, tomato, chilis, tomato paste, doubanjiang, sugar, soy sauce, Shaoxing wine, water, and lu bao packet in a pot.
3. Bring to a boil, then simmer 3-4 hours until beef is tender.
4. Cook noodles separately. Blanch bok choy in the last minute.
5. Serve noodles in bowls with broth, beef, bok choy, cilantro, scallions, and pickled mustard greens.
