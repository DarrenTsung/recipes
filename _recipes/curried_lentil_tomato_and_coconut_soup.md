---
title: Curried Lentil, Tomato, and Coconut Soup
---

## Source

<https://www.epicurious.com/recipes/food/views/curried-lentil-tomato-and-coconut-soup>

## Ingredients:

- 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 (2 1/2") piece ginger, peeled, finely grated

- 2 tbsp virgin coconut oil (or extra-virgin olive oil)
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 1 tbsp medium curry powder (or equal parts turmeric, cumin, and coriander)
- 3/4 cup red lentils
- 1 (14.5 oz) can crushed tomatoes
- 1/2 cup finely chopped cilantro, plus leaves with tender stems for serving
- Kosher salt, freshly ground pepper
- 1 (13.5 oz) can unsweetened coconut milk, shaken well
- Lime wedges, for serving

- 1 thai chili / spice source (optional)

## Notes:
- I blended the ginger / garlic into the tomatoes
- I just tied a bunch of cilantro and stewed it, pulled it out after cooking

## Instructions:

1. Heat oil in a medium saucepan over medium. Cook onion, stirring often, until softened and golden brown, 8-10 minutes. Add garlic, ginger, curry powder, and red pepper flakes; cook, stirring, until fragrant, ~2 minutes. Add lentils and cook, stirring, 1 minute.
2. Add tomatoes, 1/2 cup cilantro, a generous pinch of salt, and 1 cup water; season with pepper. Set aside 1/4 cup coconut milk for serving and add the rest to the saucepan.
3. Bring to a boil; reduce heat and simmer gently, stirring occasionally, until lentils are soft but not mushy, 20-25 minutes. Adjust salt and pepper.
4. Divide among bowls. Drizzle with reserved coconut milk, top with cilantro, and serve with lime wedges.

Do ahead: Soup (without toppings) keeps 3 days, covered and chilled.
