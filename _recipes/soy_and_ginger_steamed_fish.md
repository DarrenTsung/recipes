---
title: Soy and Ginger Steamed Fish
---

Adapted from: https://www.bonappetit.com/recipe/soy-and-ginger-steamed-fish

## Ingredients:

- 6-8 oz skinless white fish fillets (black bass, cod, snapper, or arctic char), cut into pieces
- [Curing salt](/curing_salt)
- 1 (4x3 in) piece kombu (optional)
- 2 tbsp sake
- 2 tbsp soy sauce
- 1 tbsp mirin
- 3/4 cup water
- 1/2 small napa cabbage, stems thinly sliced, leaves torn (~5 cups)
- 4 oz mixed mushrooms (shiitake, oyster, beech, maitake), torn into pieces
- 1 (2 in) piece ginger, peeled, cut into matchsticks
- 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
- 2 scallions, thinly sliced
- Cooked rice for serving

## Instructions:

1. Cure fish for 10 minutes. Wash off and pat dry.
2. Combine kombu (if using), sake, soy sauce, mirin, and water in a Dutch oven. Bring to a boil, reduce to low.
3. Layer cabbage stems, then leaves. Scatter mushrooms and ginger over, then place fish on top.
4. Cover and steam until fish is opaque, 8-10 minutes.
5. Drizzle with sesame oil, top with scallions. Serve in shallow bowls with broth spooned over and rice alongside.
